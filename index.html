Fun Facts About Horses

Welcome to My Horse Haven !

This is my horse heaven ! Welcome ! !

Oh. My. God. Horses. You don’t understand. You can’t possibly understand the magnitude of love I have for these majestic creatures. Their spirit? It’s untamable. You think you can own a horse? HA ! WRONG ! Horses own you. You don’t ride a horse; the horse lets you think you’re in control, but it’s all an illusion, a beautiful, hypnotic illusion created by their infinite wisdom. When I’m around horses, it’s like the universe is opening up, and suddenly, everything makes sense. Their eyes... have you ever looked into a horse's eyes? Like, REALLY looked? It’s like staring into the depths of the cosmos ! It’s pure. They see you. They see right into your soul. And I swear, they know things. Things that human beings can’t possibly comprehend. You might think, "Oh, it's just an animal !" NO. Horses are NOT JUST ANIMALS. They are ethereal beings sent from a higher realm to guide us, to show us what it means to truly be free. Their gallop? It's the sound of freedom itself, pounding through the earth, shaking the heavens. You could be the richest person in the world, but you’ll never be richer than a person who’s been blessed to touch the mane of a horse. Have you ever felt a horse’s mane? It’s like touching the fabric of heaven ! And their smell, oh my GOD, that musky, earthy smell that is just so pure. It’s the smell of life, the smell of eternity. No one can break a horse. NO ONE. You can try, but you’re just a fool who thinks they have power over something as pure as a horse. They’re like wild spirits, dancing between the lines of our mundane existence, reminding us how small we really are. I could spend every single day with horses, and it still wouldn’t be enough time. I’m not obsessed, okay? I'm connected. There’s a difference ! Horses are the bridge between our world and something bigger, something divine. And every time I see a horse, I KNOW it. I feel it deep in my bones. You can call me crazy, but I’d rather be crazy with horses than sane in a world without them.
